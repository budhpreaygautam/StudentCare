PHD Fellowship : Indian

1. Prime Minister Fellowship for Doctoral Research

  • • Objective : The objective of the Prime Minister's Fellowship for Doctoral Research is to support and promote high-quality research in science and technology. The program aims to nurture talent, encourage innovation, and contribute to advancements in various STEM fields by providing financial support to outstanding PhD scholars.

  • • Provider : the government of India, often under the Prime Minister's Office or a related ministry.

  • • What for : The fellowship is designed to support individuals pursuing doctoral research in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) disciplines.

  • • How many seats : 100

    > Nature of assistance

  • • Things covered : stipends for researchers, research-related expenses, contingency grants, and sometimes travel allowances for presenting research findings at conferences .

  • • Scholarship amount : Rs. 70,000-80,000.

  • • Duration : aligned with the duration of the Ph.D. program.

  • • Apply Link : www.serbficci-iirrada.in/PrimeMinisterFellowshipResearch

    > Qualifications

  • • Ranking requirement : N/A

  • • Academic qualification :

    • 1. Full time PhD registered with any academic or research institution offering PhD degree.
    • 2. Apply within 14 months of PhD admission/registration whichever date is earlier
    • 3. Complete all required academic qualification in past
    • 4. Have an industry partner to sponsor 50% of the fellowship (Rs.4lakhs per annum)

  • • Income criteria : N/A

  • • Residential requirement : Yes you hold Indian citizenship.

  • • Any other (PAN/Bonds etc.): Proof of Citizenship (birth certificate), degree certificates, etc.

    > Schedule

  • • Advertisement (prospective dates/month): May.

  • • Application procedure : Only online applications through program website will be accepted. Do not send any physical copy of the application and supporting documents. Candidates may apply for the SERB’s Prime Minister’s Fellowship for Doctoral Research Scheme at any time of the year at www.serbficci-iirrada.in.

  • • Rounds : Shortlisted candidates are invited for an interview.

  • • How to prepare the best application: N/A

  • • Things to keep in mind: N/A

2. Jawaharlal Nehru Memorial Fund Scholarships

  • • Objective : The Jawaharlal Nehru Memorial Fund Scholarships aim to provide financial support to meritorious students for pursuing higher education in India. The scholarships are designed to assist students in various academic disciplines and encourage academic excellence.

  • • Provider : Jawaharlal Nehru Memorial Fund.

  • • What for : These scholarships are awarded to students for pursuing higher studies at the postgraduate level in India.

  • • How many seats : 18 - 20.

    > Nature of assistance

  • • Things covered : tuition fees, cost of books, and other related academic costs.

  • • Scholarship amount : Between INR 15,000 to INR 18,000 per month.

  • • Duration : up to 2 years.

  • • Apply Link : www.jnmf.in/sabout.html

    > Qualifications

  • • Ranking requirement : N/A

  • • Academic qualification : Visit official website link is given above

  • • Income criteria : N/A

  • • Residential requirement : N/A

  • • Any other (PAN/Bonds etc.): Proof of Citizenship (birth certificate), degree certificates, etc.

    > Schedule

  • • Advertisement (prospective dates/month): December - May .

  • • Application procedure : Visit website

  • • Rounds : Shortlisted candidates are invited for an interview.

  • • How to prepare the best application: N/A

  • • Things to keep in mind: Age less than 35 years.

3. ICHR Junior Research Fellowships (JRF)

  • • What for : The fellowships are awarded to scholars to facilitate their research endeavors in the field of history.

  • • How many seats : N/A

  • • Duration : up to 2 years.

  • • Apply Link : junior-research-fellowship

  • • Academic qualification : Candidates must enroll for PhD in Historical Studies.

  • • Income criteria : N/A

  • • Residential requirement : Yes

  • • Any other (PAN/Bonds etc.): Proof of Citizenship (birth certificate), degree certificates, etc.

    > Schedule

  • • Advertisement (prospective dates/month): Novemeber – December .

  • • Application procedure : Visit website.

  • • Rounds : Shortlisted candidates are invited for an presentation-cum-interview.

  • • How to prepare the best application: N/A

  • • Things to keep in mind: N/A

4. Maulana Azad National Fellowship

  • • Objective : The objective of the Maulana Azad Fellowship Scheme (MANF) is to provide five year fellowships in the form of financial assistance to students from six notified minority communities viz. Buddhist, Christian, Jain, Muslim, Parsi and Sikh, notified by the Central Government, to pursue M. Phil and Ph.D.

  • • Provider : The fellowship is provided by the Ministry of Minority Affairs, Government of India.

  • • What for : The fellowship supports students pursuing M.Phil. and Ph.D. degrees by offering financial assistance to cover various academic expenses such as course fees, maintenance allowance, and other incidental charges.

  • • How many seats : vary each year.

    > Nature of assistance

  • • Things covered : The maintenance allowance is meant to cover living expenses, books, and other academic-related expenses.

  • • Scholarship amount : 28,000 per month.

  • • Duration : up to 5 years.

  • • Apply Link : www.minorityaffairs.gov.in

    > Qualifications

  • • Ranking requirement : Entrance Exam-Based (UGC NET or CSIR NET).

  • • Academic qualification : Must have completed post graduation , Candidates must enroll for PhD from a recognized University/ college.

  • • Income criteria : Family income must be less than INR 6,00,000 .

  • • Residential requirement : Yes

  • • Any other (PAN/Bonds etc.): Proof of Citizenship (birth certificate), degree certificates, etc.

    > Schedule

  • • Advertisement (prospective dates/month): twice in a year( June – December).

  • • Application procedure : Visit website.

  • • Rounds : Shortlisted candidates are invited for an interview.

  • • How to prepare the best application: N/A

  • • Things to keep in mind: N/A

5. NCERT Doctoral Fellowship for PhD

  • • Objective : The objective of the NCERT Doctoral Fellowship is to support scholars pursuing Ph.D. in Education and allied disciplines. The fellowship aims to promote research in the field of education and contribute to the improvement of the education system in India.

  • • Provider : National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT).

  • • What for : Candidates registered for a Ph.D. degree in a recognized university/ or working toward their Ph.D. registration are eligible to apply.

  • • How many seats : 10 - 15.

    > Nature of assistance

  • • Things covered : The Fellow will be provided III AC fare for the travel and accommodation in the RIE hostel.

  • • Scholarship amount : 23,000-25,000 per month.

  • • Duration : up to 3 years.

  • • Apply Link : forms.ncert.gov.in

    > Qualifications

  • • Ranking requirement : N/A

  • • Academic qualification : Good Academic record with at least 60% marks at both Graduate and Post Graduate Levels.

  • • Income criteria : N/A

  • • Residential requirement : Yes

  • • Any other (PAN/Bonds etc.): Proof of Citizenship (birth certificate), degree certificates, etc.

    > Schedule

  • • Advertisement (prospective dates/month): November – December.

  • • Application procedure : The procedure to shortlist applicants will be designed by NCERT. The shortlisted candidates will be required to appear for an interview, which may also include a presentation of the proposals.

  • • Rounds : Registration and Online Assessment, Personal Interview.

  • • How to prepare the best application: N/A

  • • Things to keep in mind: Candidates should not be more than 30 years of age as of the last date of receipt of applications.
