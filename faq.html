Scholarship FAQ's

How much amount can I receive for a scholarship?

The scholarship amount varies depending on various factors such as the specific scholarship program, your academic achievements, financial need, and the institution offering the scholarship. It is best to research and apply for scholarships to determine the exact amount you may be eligible to receive.

What are merit-based scholarships?

Merit-based scholarships are awarded based on a student’s academic, athletic, artistic, or other achievements. These scholarships recognize exceptional performance or talent in a specific area. They often require high grades, standardized test scores, or evidence of exceptional abilities.

Can I apply for multiple scholarships at the same time?

Yes. You can apply for multiple scholarships if you are fulfilling the eligibility criteria. However, you can receive only one scholarship at a time.

What are need-based scholarships?

Need-based scholarships are awarded to students who demonstrate financial need. These scholarships aim to support students who may not have the means to fund their education fully. Financial need is usually assessed based on factors such as family income, assets, and the cost of attending a particular institution.

Can I apply for a scholarship abroad?

Yes, you can apply for scholarships abroad. Many universities and organizations offer scholarships for international students to study in various countries.

What criteria should be met to be eligible for a scholarship?

The eligibility criteria for each scholarship may vary. However, it is generally based on students’ current academic qualification, family income, merit, gender, caste/category and domicile. In addition, sports-based and talent-based scholarships are also available.
